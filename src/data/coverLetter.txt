**Federal Rule of Evidence 902(14) – Self‑Authentication of Digital Evidence**

Federal Rule of Evidence 902(14) provides that data copied from an electronic device, website, or cloud service is self‑authenticating if it is certified by a qualified person and accompanied by a verified digital hash (e.g., SHA‑256) that confirms the copy has not been altered. The Witness R.E.P. Certificate, together with the SHA‑256 hash listed above and the publicly available verification tool, satisfies these requirements. Courts may admit the video and certificate into evidence without the need for live forensic testimony regarding the hash or the underlying encryption process, as long as the opposing party is given reasonable notice. This significantly streamlines the authentication process and reduces the cost and complexity of introducing smartphone video at trial.
